

CHAPTER 6 — FOUNDER EARNINGS MODEL

Aligned Incentives, Transparent Structure, and Shared Growth

The Bazaria earnings model is designed to align the Founder’s success with the region’s success.It is simple, transparent, and emotionally fair.

This chapter defines how Founders earn, how payouts work, and how the model reinforces stewardship rather than extraction.

6.1 Purpose of the Earnings Model

The earnings model exists to:

reward stewardship

incentivize growth

maintain fairness

prevent exploitation

align Founder and platform interests

support long‑term regional stability

The model is intentionally simple to avoid confusion, manipulation, or drift.

6.2 Founder Earnings Structure

Founders earn:

40% of gross profit generated within their region

Gross profit is defined as:

(Final sale price – shipping cost – payment processing fees)

This percentage is:

fixed

non‑negotiable

globally consistent

tied to performance

aligned with stewardship

6.3 Payout Frequency

Payouts occur:

monthly

automatically

through the Founder dashboard

with full ledger visibility

Founders receive:

payout summary

transaction breakdown

dispute adjustments

escrow contributions

6.4 Earnings Transparency

Founders have access to:

regional sales data

dispute adjustments

escrow contributions

payout history

performance metrics

All data is:

read‑only

tamper‑proof

ledger‑backed

6.5 Earnings Limitations

Founders may not:

alter payout percentages

negotiate special rates

bypass escrow

adjust seller fees

create local incentives

modify the earnings model

All earnings rules are global and permanent.

6.6 Earnings and Stewardship

The earnings model reinforces:

discipline

fairness

long‑term thinking

cultural stewardship

operational consistency

Founders earn more when:

disputes are low

trust is high

listings are accurate

sellers are active

buyers return

6.7 Earnings Summary

The Founder earnings model is:

simple

aligned

transparent

fair

global

non‑negotiable

It rewards stewardship, not shortcuts.

CHAPTER 7 — ESCROW GOVERNANCE

The 10% Regional Growth Fund and Its Sacred Rules

Escrow is the stabilizing force of every region.It is not a pool of money — it is a trust mechanism.

This chapter defines how escrow is created, governed, protected, and used.

7.1 Purpose of Escrow

Escrow exists to:

protect regional stability

support cultural programs

resolve disputes

fund emergencies

maintain fairness

reinforce trust

prevent misuse of funds

Escrow is the guardian fund of each region.

7.2 Escrow Contribution

Each region contributes:

10% of gross profit into a dedicated escrow account

This contribution is:

automatic

non‑negotiable

region‑specific

forward‑only

7.3 Escrow Governance Structure

Escrow is governed by:

the Regional Founder (steward)

Canon Keepers (oversight)

auditors (periodic review)

Governance must be:

transparent

documented

disciplined

culturally aligned

7.4 Approved Escrow Uses

Escrow may be used for:

1. Cultural Programs

community events

cultural celebrations

identity‑building initiatives

2. Marketplace Stabilization

dispute compensation

emergency refunds

seller support during crises

3. Regional Growth

outreach

marketing

onboarding programs

4. Crisis Response

natural disasters

political instability

economic shocks

7.5 Prohibited Escrow Uses

Escrow may never be used for:

Founder compensation

personal expenses

bonuses

loans

investments

political contributions

travel unrelated to regional operations

gifts

speculative activities

These prohibitions are absolute.

7.6 Escrow Access Protocol

Access requires:

documented request

justification

Founder approval

Canon Keeper review (for major withdrawals)

ledger entry

Escrow access must feel ceremonial, not casual.

7.7 Escrow Audits

Audits occur:

annually

during renewal

after disputes

after stabilization events

Audits ensure:

no misuse

no drift

no ambiguity

7.8 Escrow Violations

Violations include:

unauthorized withdrawals

personal use

undocumented usage

concealment

Consequences may include:

probation

removal

territory reassignment

ledger annotation

7.9 Escrow Summary

Escrow is:

sacred

protected

documented

audited

community‑oriented

It is the financial backbone of regional trust.
